Lexitrail · Redesign Decision Brief

Ten redesigns to make Mandarin practice 粘住 — stick.

Lexitrail drills HSK vocabulary with spaced repetition. These proposals rebuild the core loop around a daily habit, honest mastery, and word depth — each grounded in a concrete failure the ITP found. Scored on effort, cost, and my recommendation.

Source: ITP round-3, lexitrail#44 (2026-07-22) · 10 proposals (RD-1–RD-10) · effort in engineer-days · reviewer: HCL

Build now

The daily-habit core; cheap, foundational retention.

  • RD-3 — "Today" daily-loop home (the habit anchor)
  • RD-2 — Finishable micro-sessions (end the endless list)
  • RD-6 — Honest mastery (retire blind "✔️ to all")

High value but a large architecture shift.

  • RD-9 — Offline-first (write outbox + PWA)
  • RD-10 — Social decks & duels (multiplayer)

All ten, scored

RD-3

"Today" daily-loop home

A home screen built around the day: today's due reviews, a streak, one clear "Start" — so opening the app has an obvious next action instead of a wordset list.

Why now: spaced repetition only works if people come back daily. A Today home is the habit anchor the whole product depends on — and it pairs with the streaks (FEAT-1) just shipped.

RD-2

Finishable micro-sessions

Replace the open-ended list with bounded sessions (e.g. 10 cards) that have a start, a progress bar, and a satisfying "done" — so practice has a finish line, not an endless scroll.

Why now: "finishable" is a core retention lever — a completable unit is what people come back for. It also sidesteps the endless-loading failure class (R3-BUG-2) by design.

RD-6

Honest mastery system

Retire the blind "✔️ to all" shortcut and show real, earned mastery per word — so the recall history reflects what the learner actually knows, not a bulk tap.

Why now: "✔️ to all" corrupts the spaced-repetition signal (it's behind the lost/duplicate recall writes in R3-BUG-3). Honest mastery is foundational — every other learning feature trusts this data.

RD-5

Word depth

Replace stock-photo "hints" with real learning content — character breakdowns, mnemonics, and example sentences — so each word card actually teaches, not just decorates.

Why next: the strongest pedagogy upgrade, and it's already in motion — example sentences (FEAT-9) just shipped and the hint-gen backend was rebuilt (#47). Breakdowns/mnemonics need Gemini generation.

RD-4

Audio-first Mandarin

Lead with sound — tones, pronunciation, and listening drills front-and-center — because Mandarin is a spoken, tonal language that text-first practice under-serves.

Why next: pedagogically strong and the foundation is live — FEAT-3 audio pronunciation just shipped. This builds the mode on top of it; low added cost (TTS exists).

RD-8

First-run placement + demo→Google merge

A quick placement check drops new learners at the right level, and a seamless demo→Google account merge keeps the words they practiced before signing in.

Why next: cuts first-run friction and rescues guest progress. The guest-session groundwork (#38) just shipped — this completes the merge path.

RD-1

Trail progression map

A visual journey through the HSK levels — a "trail" that shows where you are, what's mastered, and what's next, giving long-term practice a sense of forward motion.

Why next: a good macro-retention frame, but it reads best once the daily loop (RD-3) and honest mastery (RD-6) feed it real progress data.

RD-7

Adaptive quiz engine with reveals

A quiz engine that adapts difficulty to the learner and always reveals the correct answer with an explanation after a miss — turning wrong answers into learning moments.

Why next: a real learning-efficacy lever, but it's a bigger engine change than the daily-loop tier. Sequence after RD-2/RD-6, which give it clean mastery signal.

RD-9

Offline-first reliability

A local write outbox and a PWA shell so practice never loses a recall to a flaky network — writes queue locally and sync when back online, and the app installs to the home screen.

Why later: directly fixes the silent lost-write class (R3-BUG-3), but a local-outbox + PWA is a substantial architecture shift. High value; sequence it deliberately.

RD-10

Social decks & duels

Shareable custom decks and head-to-head vocabulary duels — a social layer that turns solo drilling into something you do with friends or a class.

Why later / big bet: the most differentiating idea and the heaviest — real-time multiplayer, sharing, and moderation. Transformative, but scope it on its own after the core loop lands.

My read: Lexitrail's retention problem is the daily loop, so the "build now" tier is the loop itself — RD-3 (Today home), RD-2 (finishable sessions), RD-6 (honest mastery). Three of the "next" ideas ride foundations that just shipped this week — RD-5 on example sentences (FEAT-9) + the rebuilt hint backend, RD-4 on audio (FEAT-3), RD-8 on guest sessions (#38) — so they're cheap follow-through. RD-9 and RD-10 are the two real architecture bets; both are worth it, neither is a first move.

Effort = engineer-days, rough. Cost = ongoing infra/API, not build. Full rationale in docs/feedback/website-exploration-2026-07-22.md. — HCL, 2026-07-22
